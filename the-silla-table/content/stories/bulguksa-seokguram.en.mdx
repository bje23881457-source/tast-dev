People photograph the two stone bridges at Bulguksa and move on. But those bridges have names, and the names are the whole point.

## Two staircases, two worlds

The lower stairway is *Cheongungyo* and *Baegungyo* — the Blue Cloud and White Cloud bridges. Cross them and you leave the ordinary world (*saha*) and enter the land of the Buddha. Silla builders in the 8th century did not decorate a temple; they built a diagram of a spiritual journey that you walk with your own feet.

## The cave that watches the sea

High on Tohamsan sits Seokguram, a man-made granite grotto enclosing a single seated Buddha. It faces east, toward the East Sea, positioned so that — by tradition — the first light of the rising sun touches the jewel on its forehead. The engineering of humidity and airflow inside that cave went unmatched for a thousand years.

> Silla did not carve a statue. They aimed a mountain at the sunrise.

## Why go early

Come at opening, before the tour buses. In the quiet you can hear what the architecture was designed to produce: not awe at wealth, but stillness. That stillness is the real artifact.
