Two days is enough to feel Gyeongju, not to exhaust it. Here is a rhythm that works.

## Day one — the old capital

- **Morning:** The royal tomb park and Cheomseongdae observatory on foot, while the light is soft.
- **Midday:** Lunch and a slow tea break; the heat and the crowds both peak now.
- **Evening:** Wolji Pond as the lamps come on. This is the day's centrepiece — take a night walk and let a host tell you the stories the plaques leave out.

## Day two — mountain and meaning

- **Early morning:** Bulguksa and Seokguram before the buses, up Tohamsan.
- **Afternoon:** The national museum to connect what you have seen to what was found.
- **Evening:** A seasonal dinner to close — the flavours of the court you spent two days walking through.

## The one rule

Do less than you think you should. Gyeongju rewards attention, not mileage.
