Gyeongju changes after sunset. The crowds thin, the monuments light up, and the city finally feels its age. This walk is how I like to introduce guests to it.

## What we do

We begin at Wolji Pond as the lamps come on, then move through Donggung palace grounds and along the old royal axis toward Cheomseongdae, the 7th-century observatory. Between sites we pause for tea and I tell the stories the plaques leave out.

## The pace is the point

This is not a march. We stop, we look, we let a place settle before moving on. Expect roughly two hours and easy, flat walking.

## Good to know

- **Group size:** 2–8 guests, kept small on purpose.
- **When:** Evenings, year-round; loveliest in spring and autumn.
- **Language:** Hosted in fluent English (Korean on request).

Inquire with your dates and group size and we will tailor the route.
