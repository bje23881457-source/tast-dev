People ask me this more than anything else: "Where should I actually stay?" The honest answer is that it depends on how you like to spend your evenings. Gyeongju is small, but the three main areas feel like different towns.

## The three areas, plainly

- **The historic old town (near Daereungwon and Hwangnidan-gil):** My default recommendation. You can walk to the royal tombs, Cheomseongdae, and the cafe street. Guesthouses and small hotels, not luxury, but you step out the door and you're already there.
- **Bomun Lake resort area:** Big hotels, spa pools, room to breathe. Lovely and calm, but it's a 15-minute taxi from the old town, so you commit to driving or buses for sightseeing.
- **Near Singyeongju (KTX) station:** Practical if you arrive late or leave early. Newer business hotels, but honestly a little soulless and 20 minutes from the sights.

## Is a hanok stay worth it?

Yes, once. Sleeping on a warm ondol floor inside a tiled-roof house, courtyard quiet all around you, is the kind of memory that outlasts the trip. Just know the trade-offs: thin walls, floor bedding, shared bathrooms in older places. Book the ones near the old town so you're not stranded.

> If it's your first visit, stay in the old town. You'll thank yourself when you wander out after dark and everything is a short walk away.

## My rule of thumb

First-timers: old town. Couples wanting rest: Bomun Lake. One night in transit: the station. And leave at least one evening free to simply walk — the tombs and Cheomseongdae glow after sunset, and that unhurried night stroll is the thing most guests remember most.
